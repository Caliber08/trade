It Seems

Current MMOs are crowded with characters who are deadlocked on a personal storyline. The MUDs of old encouraged users to interact and contribute not just in a specific quest or raid but for mutual benefit and social fulfillment. As chat rooms became fantastic worlds we see more and more of our avatars and less and less of each other.

Trade is a game that allows a user to coach a character (or set of characters) through advanced development. It is, in part, a survival game, but seeks to be a game in which characters find new ways to thrive.

Basics

All entities in the Trade world act with an intelligence. Each strives to survive, grow, and thrive based on some universal goals and a few specific ones. When a user is playing, her will suppresses (to some extent) the will of the active character.

Survive
Every creature has basic needs for food, water, shelter, and good health.
Grow
Plants and animals increase their characteristics over time.
Thrive
Each species is driven by desires and may form packs, avoid conflict, amass wealth, etc.
